## January 2026

- [Attention Is All You Need](https://arxiv.org/abs/1706.03762) — Vaswani, Shazeer, Parmar, Uszkoreit, Jones, Gomez, Kaiser, Polosukhin (2017)
  > *We propose a new simple network architecture, the Transformer, based solely on attention mechanisms, dispensing with recurrence and convolutions entirely.*

- [Deep Residual Learning for Image Recognition](https://arxiv.org/abs/1512.03385) — He, Zhang, Ren, Sun (2015)
  > *We present a residual learning framework to ease the training of networks that are substantially deeper than those used previously.*

- [BERT: Pre-training of Deep Bidirectional Transformers](https://arxiv.org/abs/1810.04805) — Devlin, Chang, Lee, Toutanova (2018)
  > *We introduce a new language representation model called BERT, which stands for Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers.*

## December 2025

- [Language Models are Few-Shot Learners](https://arxiv.org/abs/2005.14165) — Brown, Mann, Ryder, Subbiah, Kaplan (2020)
  > *We demonstrate that scaling up language models greatly improves task-agnostic, few-shot performance.*

- [ImageNet Classification with Deep Convolutional Neural Networks](https://papers.nips.cc/paper/4824-imagenet-classification-with-deep-convolutional-neural-networks) — Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Hinton (2012)
  > *We trained a large, deep convolutional neural network to classify the 1.2 million images in the ImageNet LSVRC-2010 contest.*

- [Generative Adversarial Networks](https://arxiv.org/abs/1406.2661) — Goodfellow, Pouget-Abadie, Mirza, Xu, Warde-Farley, Ozair, Courville, Bengio (2014)
  > *We propose a new framework for estimating generative models via an adversarial process.*

## November 2025

- [Adam: A Method for Stochastic Optimization](https://arxiv.org/abs/1412.6980) — Kingma, Ba (2014)
  > *We introduce Adam, an algorithm for first-order gradient-based optimization of stochastic objective functions.*

- [Dropout: A Simple Way to Prevent Neural Networks from Overfitting](https://jmlr.org/papers/v15/srivastava14a.html) — Srivastava, Hinton, Krizhevsky, Sutskever, Salakhutdinov (2014)
  > *We propose dropout, a technique for addressing overfitting in neural networks by randomly dropping units during training.*
